My Story

As a former teacher and composer, I was nervous about moving into a new field like software development, scared of losing the things I loved about working in education and music. But what I have discovered is a new passion that shares so many of the things I love about my previous experiences. While working as a freelance composer I spent my time in meetings trying to understand what my client really wanted for their product and then hours planning how to build what they needed. This is a process that I loved and continue to love through the filter of software development. I love the challenge that each project brings, working with others, and planing ways to deliver the best products. As an educator I went to conventions and constantly tried new tools to teach my students in the most effective ways to produce the best results. Now, as a developer, I continue to practice those same skills by constantly learning new solutions or exploring new frameworks to solve problems and implement tools. I'm excited to finally find a passion in development, a field that seems to be limitless in its depth and potential to help others.

DIGITizeclose

This application is designed to help therapists complete exercises with their patients to build fine motor skills after brain or spinal cord injuries and keep track of their patient's progress through their recovery. This application was made with AngularJS and Firebase. This project was created working with user interviews with a therapist from Skyline Medical Center in Nashville, TN. Only non-critical data is stored in this application to keep patient information as secure as possible. The game was designed to give users as much positive feedback as possible by reacting with visual and audio cues in the application. Accessibility was also very important in creating this application by making sure colors and displays were suitable for users with visual impairments. Games and patient records are stored and manipulated as arrays of objects via Firebase and passed from controllers by factories that record new games without overwriting previous game histories. I also created all of the music and sounds for this game.

Musixxxclose

This application was created to let a user preview music from the iTunes store and add then to their personal wish list. This app was created with Firebase, AngularJS, and Heroku. The authentication for this project is through Firebase. The user's account information is stored in a database on Firebase by data-binding through AngularJS. The application is connected to the iTunes open API and could even be extended through the iTune Affiliate Program to actually generate revenue. There is also a how to for creating a proxy-server that I used to solve CORS problems in the README.md of this repository.

Robot BattleDomeclose

This application lets users build a robot and battle against foes. This application uses a rock, paper, scissors battle system and a progressive leveling system that is coded into the Javascript logic in the battle controller. This project was built with AngularJS and MaterializeCss. There are bosses every 5 levels that feature some of my mentors while learning these concepts. The heath and damage are calculated live and displayed in the combat log and their health bars. I also created all of the music for the game myself.

Previous Works

written/arr. by Blaise Roberts

  • menuFinal Fantasy for Front Ensemble
    This is an arrangement for marimba and vibraphone created for the Hebron HS front ensemble in 2011 that features a few different tunes from the Final Fantasy Series. This arrangment was quite challenging and was made to push some of the more advanced kids in the program.
  • menuPokémon Battle for Front Ensemble
    This is an arrangement for marimba and vibraphone created for the Fossil Ridge HS front ensemble in 2012 that features the battle theme from Pokémon. This arrangment was a two mallet speed exercise with emphasis on chromatic passages.
  • menuWake for HS Marching Band
    This is an arrangement/composition for high school marching band. This show was titled 'Wake' and took the audience on a journey through dealing with the grief of a death. The journey moves from a place of darkness into light and features quotes from many differnt sources spanning from 16th century counterpoint to modern orchestral selections. The music for the battery percussion was written by Ben Moore.
  • menuWintersprints for HS Marching Band
    This is an arrangement/composition for the last movement in a high school marching band show. This show was titled 'aleaffalls' and focused on the transition from Autumn into Winter. This movement is the beginning of winter and features selecctions from Vivaldi's string quartet as well as a concert band piece by Richard Saucedo called 'Windsprints' The music for the battery percussion was written by Ben Moore.
